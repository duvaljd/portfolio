Design Philosophy

The most important part of design is empathy.

You'll never hear me refer to people as "users" because I'm always thinking about them beyond their interactions with an interface. I'm committed to using forward-thinking technology, beautiful design, and clear writing to empower people to accomplish good things. Considering people in context—who they are, what they care about, and what they want to do—is integral to effective design.

I think a good designer always practices empathy. They dsicover people's needs (stated, observed, anticipated); take the time to understand the larger contexts in which people live, play, and work; and remain open to solutions that aren't always intuitive to themselves. Investing first and foremost in empathizing with people and their goals allows a designer to create solutions that accommodate people, rather than asking people to accommodate solutions—and these, I think, are most effective.

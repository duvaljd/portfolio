Jibe

Recruiting Cloud

Needs Assessment and Usability Evaluation

As part of my coursework as a graduate student at the University of Michigan School of Information, I was a member of a student group tasked with conducting a needs assessment and usability evaluation of a specific instance of Jibe's career site and job search platform.

Over the course of four months, we used several methods to discover the needs of people using Jibe's platform and to evaluate the platform itself:

  • Created an interaction map for the platform;
  • Conducted interviews with job seekers to better understand the people who use Jibe's platform;
  • Designed and implemented a survey to verify the conclusions from our interviews;
  • Completed a comparative analysis to identify popular features and capabilities missing from the platform;
  • Completed a heuristic analysis of the site, focusing on 10 key criteria;
  • Designed and executed usability tests with five recruited participants.

We summarized our findings in a video report, which identified the key areas Jibe should focus their energies on addressing with future revisions of the platform. All deliverables from this project are available upon request.

Pillar Technologies

The Forge

Makerspace Feasability Study

As part of my coursework as a graduate student at the University of Michigan School of Information, I was a member of a student consultant group tasked with assisting a local technology group, Pillar Technologies, with identifying possible uses for under-utilized office space.

Over the course of four months, we engaged in research and used contextual inquiry methods to produce a set of recommendations for Pillar Technology to consider as they considered how to use their office space:

  • Conducted research on Pillar Technology, including their market position, office culture, and role in the Ann Arbor tech community;
  • Conducted research on DIY/makerspace communities and the role and effects of corporate makerspaces;
  • Created interview protocols and conducted interviews with Pillar Technology employees;
  • Recounted and reviewed interview sessions in interpretation sessions;
  • Created an affinity wall to organize and analyze interview notes and insights;
  • Developed recommendations based on research and interview.

The recommendations ranged from high-level suggestions for addressing restricted communication flows, to low-cost considerations for addressing slowed decision-making around transforming the office space.

Contextual Inquiry

Addressing organizational challenges using employee interviews, research, and card sorting

During this externship, I worked on a consulting team for Pillar Technology, gathering data and offering recommendations about how they utilized the space of their offices at The Forge in Ann Arbor, Michigan. Our team conducted background research on the organization, interviews with staff and management, and a competitive analysis, then used card sorting methodology to analyze our data.

The resulting report includes recommendations on how Pillar can better utilize space within the Forge and suggestions on how management can better tap into the great ideas permeating Pillar's offices, all supported by data from sources both internal and external to Pillar.

Website Redesign

Modernizing a university website (design exercise)

For this design exercise during my graduate coursework, I conducted a peer review to identify the design and information organization trends popular among other information schools, and used these findings to create mockups of a redesign for the University of Michigan School of Information home page.

During the peer review, I was drawn to sites that featured edge-to-edge images and colors, with bold typography, generous white space, and clear visual heirarchy. I brought these practices to the UMSI website, creating clear visual heirarchies with lots of breathing room, using Michigan's signature maize and blue colors to draw attention to headers, and choosing typefaces that align with Michigan's style guide while maintaining boldness (Benton Sans) and readability (Minion Pro).

Usability Evaluation

Discovering user needs and evaluating usability for a popular careers web platform

During this externship, I worked on a consulting team for Jibe—specifcally, on the job application submission process of their recruiting cloud platform. Our team conducted a series of surveys, interviews, and usability tests with potential users; researched competitors; and performed evaluative tests against Nielsen's heuristics for interface design.

The resulting series of reports includes a journey map of the application process; transcripts of interviews; and the results from our survey, heuristic evaluation, and usability testing, including personas and scenarios. Our team also used our scenarios and persons to produce a video walkthrough of our recommendations for increasing the usability of the platform.

Process Map

Mapping how people move through a client recruitment process

I created this process map to capture the journey applicants go through to become a client for the University of Michigan School of Information's client-based courses. As the client recruitment team transitions to using a new project management application, the map helps us identify where we can and should alter the client recruitment process to take advantage of the capabilities of the new application to streamline and simplify the process for clients.
